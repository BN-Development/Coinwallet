What is Ethereum?

Bitcoin is the original blockchain-based digital currency. It was originated by Satoshi Nakamoto, the mind behind blockchain, as an anonymous, decentralized alternative to traditional currencies.

Bitcoin can be sent from user to user, traded for other crypto or fiat assets, or stored like any traditional financial asset. The difference is that Bitcoin is a peer-to-peer currency. That means no reliance on central banks or intermediaries.

Bitcoin is produced by miners who mine Bitcoin blocks on the blockchain. Its mining cap is set at 21 million coins to protect the value of its tokens.

What is Ether?

Ether is the coin mined on the Ethereum cryptocurrency. Instead of being purely a currency, like other major coins, Ether coins are used to fuel the activities of the Ethereum blockchain. Ether assets can be stored in an Ethereum wallet.

There is no set limit on Ether production and its circulation is currently over 100M
